Stanford Languages Club

Mission statement

The Stanford Languages Club is a platform for people to indulge their interest in foreign languages and in language-learning. We practice and develop a game-based suite of accelerated-language-acquisition techniques Where Are Your Keys. Through this system, people can learn languages from and teach languages to others primarily using concrete props, sign language, and a thrilling learning atmosphere rather than using rote memorization or translations. In addition, we provide a forum for appreciating the diversity of natural and constructed languages and the linguistic features they employ. We invite people of all studies to join us as long as they have a zest for language-learning, education, psychology, or linguistics.

Radio

    Isn't multilingual radio great? Yes. Yes it is. Here are some stations we listen to.

  • Swedish (Svenska)

    Sveriges Radio We recommend P1 to get mostly speech, especially the program Språket about Swedish language, and Radio Sweden på lätt svenska, the news program in simple Swedish. Also check out the various P4 channels for a taste of some different dialects!

    P1 Direkt:

  • Norwegian (Norsk)

    Again the Norwegian national radio station NRK is good. The language program is Språkteigen. Another nice program I frequently listen to is Ekko. Also check out the Folk Music program NRK Folkemusikk for some sweet hardingfele (Norwegian fiddle) and mouth harp music.

  • Spanish (Español/Castellano)

    I usually listen to Madrid News, but there are of course lots of other programs / Spanish regional news stations there on the Spanish national radio station. We welcome recommendations for radio from other countries / other dialects of Spanish!

  • French (Français)

    I typically listen to the Journal en Français Facile. Suggestions for more advanced programs and Quebecois programs welcome.

    Not exactly a radio, but Ça se passe la haut has nice astro articles read slowly and well-articulated as well as the full text.

    Also, for some Swiss people having fun with their four languages, see Parlez-vous Suisse ? on RTS.

  • German (Deutsch)

    If you're interested in the German in Switzerland, check out SRF. You may notice the Kultur channel is always in Hochdeutsch.

  • Translingual

    Also, there's a pretty cool site, radio.garden, with webradios from all over the world on a big map. Of course, you should be ready to expect a fair amount of American pop music.
